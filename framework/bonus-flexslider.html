## The Thumbnail Slider What if you wanted to have all your images to have a small row of thumbnails along the bottom to help with navigation for the user? You can [see an example of this here](http://flexslider.woothemes.com/thumbnail-slider.html). The steps for this are to create 2 seperate flexsliders. 1 is the **carousel**, the thumbnails at the bottom, and the other is the main rotator. We create both of them and then give the JS the right commands to work for both of them and keep them in sync.
## The Randomness What if we are showing a bunch of slides from different artists and we don't want to show preference to one artist over another? ```javascript $('.flexslider').flexslider({ randomize: true }); ``` Boom! impartial.
## Clickable Slides It is super simple to make your slides able to link to another page, or even another section on the same page. Let's look at the HTML we are using now: ```xml <div id="rotator"> <ul class="slides"> <li><img src="img/rotator/image1.jpg" alt="First Slide"></li> <li><img src="img/rotator/image2.jpg" alt="Second Slide"></li> <li><img src="img/rotator/image3.jpg" alt="Third Slide"></li> </ul> </div> ``` Now let's just wrap those images in links using the ```<a>``` tag in **HTML** ```xml <div id="rotator"> <ul class="slides"> <li><a href="#section1"><img src="img/rotator/image1.jpg" alt="First Slide"></a></li> <li><a href="#section2"><img src="img/rotator/image2.jpg" alt="Second Slide"></a></li> <li><a href="#section3"><img src="img/rotator/image3.jpg" alt="Third Slide"></a></li> </ul> </div> ```
## Something that's not an image? Flexslider works by rotating between slides *regardless of their content*. So you could easily do something like this: ```xml <div id="rotator"> <ul class="slides"> <li><p>Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet</p></li> <li><p>consectetur adipisicing elit.</p></li> <li><p>Impedit, ullam iste pariatur enim consequuntur.</p></li> </ul> </div> ```
